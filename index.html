ISE Admission Website

Previous Next


 This website was used for new applicants to the International School of Engineering in Chulalongkorn University to select their desired major. They can sign in to the website using their email and a password randomly generated and given by the faculty. In the website, they must rank the majors they want the most and finally, print out a generated form to hand in during their final interview with the faculty.
 At the day of the interview, the new applicants will be shown a queue and the number of remaining seats in each major projected onto a large screen in their waiting room. When it is their turn to the interview, the interviewer (professor) will have his own laptop displaying a user interface showing the status of the interviewee (student). The interface will show the professor the ranking of the student, their selected majors, and buttons for when the student makes a decision. The student may accept or decline their assigned major. Additionally, the professor can set the status of the student as late in case they fail to attend the interview on time.
 This was a extracurricular project assigned to me and 6 other students during my second year at CU, 2015. I was in the back-end team. It was run in a remote linux server accessed with SSH and FTP running Apache. The server was implemented with PHP for the front-end team to access back-end functionalities and used SQL with phpMyAdmin for the database. The server also uses SSL for encryption and is used only one month each year.

Bear Game

Previous Next


 This is one of my first 3D Unity games. It is a local multiplayer FPS game implemented using UNET HLAPI, where the game can be played through LAN. The player can control their character and shoot each other in a free for all gamemode. The map is inspired from Call of Duty 4’s multiplayer map ‘Shipment’. There are also powerups around the map to increase the player’s health, refill their ammunition, and make them turn invisible for small amount of time.
 To play the game, the host must start the game instance where their IP address and port will be visible for the clients to join. The clients then type in the IP address of the host and then they may join the game.
 For this project, I worked as a programmer in a group of 3. I was mainly responsible for coding eg character controls, character interactions and networking logic. My colleagues were responsible for the character model and animation and the map itself.

Alien Command

Previous Next


 AlienCommand is a fast paced online multiplayer game playable on Android and iOS. Players can simply download the apk of the game and start playing. The game basically works like rock-paper-scissors. The player can summon a color coded unit from their base and the unit will automatically walk towards the opponent. The units will interact with each other in the middle of the screen. Once the unit reaches the opponent’s base, the opponent will take damage. The player that is the first to destroy the opponent’s base will be the winner. The game also has a training mode where the player can face off against three different levels of AI opponents.
 The game features automatic matchmaking using a customized script with Unity’s HLAPI. The networking code of the game is also optimized to be extremely lightweight and event based rather than constant synchronization. The game also has monetized advertisements from Google’s AdMob and a button to donate to the developers via an in-app purchase. The art assets of the game were imported for free with CC0 1.0. This included both ingame characters and menu UI elements. It was developed in a team of 2 in Chulalongkorn University, Faculty of Engineering, Game Laboratories, 2017.
 The game was first prototyped in 3D but later redesigned into 2D to better suit touchscreen interactions of smartphones. At first, it was tested with multiple kinds of units eg slow but tough or fast but weak. Eventually, through testing, it was redesigned to a rock-paper-scissors system for simplicity and to fit the development time frame. The UI was also updated multiple times and also had to be made responsive to support different screen sizes.

Pick Me Up

Previous Next


 This game is a simple 3D simulation of a physical claw machine. The purpose of the game is to pick up as many items as possible during the limited time. Many kinds of animals will drop from the top of the machine for the player to pick up. The player can try to pick up the hourglass to extend their game time. At the end of the game, the player will get a chance to submit their score onto Firebase in a leaderboard.
 To control the claw, the player can use “WASD” or the arrow keys. Use “space” to lower and raise the claw. Use “Ctrl” to open or close the claw. Use “Q” and “E” to rotate the camera.
 I worked in a group of 4 at National Taiwan University. I delegated work across my team as they are from different fields. In the group, we separated into designer, art, UI, and gameplay programming. Once again, I assigned myself as the gameplay programmer. I worked closely with the UI designer as the project requires integration between my gameplay implementation and his implementation of Firebase into Unity.

Mad Cock

Previous Next


 Mad Cock is a top-down twin-stick shooter game where the players control the survivors that join forces as the story progresses. In each stage, the players will have to fight against an onslaught of MAD cocks (chickens) and complete certain objectives to advance to the next stage. As the players progress, the difficulty will increase as the cocks will become more diverse, possessing more unique abilities and the objectives of each stage will become more challenging as well. This brings a teamwork aspect to the game, where players that leverage character skills efficiently between teammates will have an easier time clearing the latter stages.
 I started working on Mad Cock at the start of 2018. During the time, I worked as a part-time programmer for MAD VR Studios and took on the project. From then on, I worked on all technical aspects including but not limited to gameplay programming, network programming, and AI programming as the sole programmer of the game. Additionally, I had the opportunity to communicate and work closely with multiple artists on the team such as the 2D and 3D artists, techinal artist, and sound artist. Furthermore, the game was also on display at BIDC Showcase 2018.
 Although the game was archived after not receiving enough funding from Kickstarter, working on this game was an eye-opening experience and in the end, the game was in a playable state where we were able to enjoy it within the studio.
